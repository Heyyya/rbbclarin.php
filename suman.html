Suman Capital of The Philippines

Clarin, a town located in Misamis Occidental, has earned the title Suman Capital of the Philippines due to its long-standing tradition of producing high-quality suman. Suman is a popular Filipino rice cake made from glutinous rice, often wrapped in banana leaves, and sometimes served with sugar, coconut, or other toppings. It’s a common treat served during festivals, special occasions, or even as a snack.

Clarin’s reputation as the Suman Capital can be traced back to its rich agricultural environment. The fertile lands of Clarin are ideal for growing glutinous rice, one of the main ingredients for making suman. The town has honed the craft of suman-making, and over the years, it has become synonymous with this delicacy. The unique preparation methods and ingredients used in Clarin's suman have set it apart from suman made in other parts of the country.

The Suman Festival

To honor its cultural heritage and promote the town's famous suman, Clarin hosts an annual event called the Suman Festival. This festival is a celebration of the town’s pride in its suman-making tradition. The festival includes a variety of activities, such as suman-making contests, cultural performances, and culinary displays. Local residents, visitors, and tourists gather to enjoy and celebrate the delicious suman, as well as the town's other cultural traditions.

Why Clarin is Known for Suman

Several factors contribute to Clarin's reputation as the Suman Capital:

  • Quality of Ingredients: The town's farmers grow the best glutinous rice, which is a key ingredient in making suman. The local rice is known for its stickiness and texture, making it ideal for this delicacy.
  • Traditional Recipes: The suman made in Clarin is prepared using traditional methods passed down through generations. Many families in Clarin have perfected their suman recipes, which are often kept as closely guarded secrets, giving the suman its unique taste.
  • Cultural Significance: Suman is deeply tied to Filipino culture, and in Clarin, it is an essential part of daily life, as well as a symbol of local pride. Suman is not just a food item but a part of the town’s identity.
  • Economic Impact: The production and sale of suman significantly contribute to the local economy. Many families rely on suman-making as a source of livelihood, and Clarin’s suman has become a source of regional and national pride, attracting tourists and food lovers.
