Clarin, Misamis Occidental

Located in Northern Mindanao, Philippines

Clarin is a municipality located in the province of Misamis Occidental in the northern part of Mindanao, Philippines. Known for its agricultural richness, cultural heritage, and picturesque landscapes, Clarin is a growing community with a blend of tradition and progress. The land area of Clarin, Misamis Occidental, covers approximately 150.62 square kilometers (or about 58.1 square miles).

Topography and Land Features

The land features of the Municipality are characterized by low flat elevations in the urban areas. The coastline portion of the Barangays of Mialen, Poblacion, Dela Paz, Lupagan, and Lapasan consists of alluvial soils and some swamp lands with an average elevation of 2.1 meters above sea level. Two major rivers cut across the town: the Clarin River in the northern portion and the Labo River in the southern part.

Geology

On the eastern part of the Municipality is a flat alluvial plain, which starts in the coastal area to inland covered with lateritic soil with numerous weathered and un-weathered boulders of basalt. This plain is made up of recent alluvium and soils derived from igneous and metamorphic rocks. On the western portion is a high mountainous belt made up of volcanic rocks, where basalt predominates.

Climates

The rainiest months of this area are November and December, and the driest months are from February to April. The average monthly rainfall is 17.3 cm. Like the Province of Misamis Occidental, the town is typhoon-free. The temperature in lowland areas is fairly warm due to the proximity of these areas to the sea, while the temperature of the barangays in the western side is noted to be cool and chilly. Relative humidity varies between 80% each month.

Soil Types and Vegetation

The area has five general types of soil as follows:

  • Bantog clay in the Poblacion
  • Lapasan
  • Segatic
  • Guimkaloan clay loam
  • Udtuyan clay loam in the interior portion
  • Mountain soils in the forest areas
  • Valencia clay loam (for rice areas)
